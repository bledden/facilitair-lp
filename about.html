About FACILITAIR

Helping AI models work together to reduce hallucinations and condense workflow clutter
since July 2025 - Made in San Francisco, CA

Our Mission

FACILITAIR is dedicated to proving that multi-agent AI collaboration isn't just theory—it's a practical solution to the hallucination problem plaguing single-model AI systems.

Through rigorous benchmarking and production deployments, we've demonstrated that specialized AI agents working together outperform generalist single-model approaches by 36.8% on average, with even greater improvements in complex domains like security and algorithm design.

The Journey

The Problem

AI hallucinations—confident but incorrect outputs—represent one of the biggest barriers to deploying AI in production. Single-model systems, no matter how sophisticated, lack the cross-validation and error-correction mechanisms that multi-agent systems provide.

The Research

We started with a simple hypothesis: if we let specialized AI agents collaborate—each focusing on what they do best—would we get better results than a single generalist model?

The answer, proven across 50,000+ inference runs testing dozens of models over three months, 100+ benchmarked tasks across 5 categories, and 62+ real-world production deployments, is a resounding yes. Sequential collaboration (Architect → Coder → Reviewer → Refiner → Documenter) achieved a 73% pass rate versus just 19% for single-model approaches—a +284% improvement in production-ready code quality.

The Validation

We didn't stop at theory. CodeSwarm and Anomaly Hunter are production systems demonstrating the real-world viability of multi-agent collaboration:

  • CodeSwarm: 92-96/100 quality scores in production code generation
  • Anomaly Hunter: 100% detection rate across 5 industries
  • Continuous Learning: Systems that improve with every task
